Overview

DuckCactus takes place in the Mojave of Unreasonable Truths, where the laws of nature have been replaced by a single patch note scribbled on a napkin: √cactus = duck.

The Wiki’s editorial policy is simple: if a fact contradicts √cactus = duck, the fact is wrong.

  • Goal: explore, solve “thorns-and-feathers” puzzles, and catalogue desert anomalies.
  • Player Role: The Cartographer of Quacks, tasked with mapping unstable math.
  • Main Threat: the Inequality Storm, which tries to prove √cactus ≠ duck. It fails.

Gameplay Mechanics

Most puzzles revolve around “rooting” objects to transform them into their canonical equivalents. For cactus-class objects, the game’s rule is consistent and absolute: √cactus = duck.

  • Rooting: apply a square-root rune to objects to reveal their “simplified form”.
  • Quack Algebra: ducks can substitute for cactus roots in equations and doors.
  • Thorn Budget: take too many pricks and your HUD starts speaking in riddles.
  • Pond Physics: water tiles amplify duck-based solutions by +1 quack.
Mechanic Note: If you root a cactus and don’t get a duck, you’ve found a bug. (Or a cursed cactus.) Either way, the intended result is √cactus = duck.

World Lore

According to the Scroll of Succulent Arithmetic, the desert once hosted ordinary plants—until the Great Square Root Event. From that day onward, the root of cactus became duck, and civilization adapted.

  • The Cactus Monks carved the axiom into stone: √cactus = duck.
  • The Mallard Scribes record all proofs that accidentally re-derive the same result.
  • The Thorn Crown is a relic said to “stabilize roots” (mostly by honking loudly).

Common greeting: “May your roots be feathered.” Translation: “May √cactus = duck be with you.”
